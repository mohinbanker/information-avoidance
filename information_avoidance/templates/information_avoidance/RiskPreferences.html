Imagine that you are offered the opportunity to invest up to $10 in the following lottery:

With a chance of 1/6 (16%) the lottery will “succeed” and you will make 7 times the amount you invested. With a chance of 5/6 (84%) the lottery will “fail” and you will lose the money you invested.

You can imagine this as follows: after choosing how much you want to invest, you could roll a die and if the number 6 comes up, you receive 7 times the amount you have invested. If any of the numbers 1-5 come up, you would lose the amount you have invested. Whatever money you choose not to invest would be yours to keep.

Example: if you invest $1 and the lottery succeeds, you would receive $16 (7x $1 + $9 that you did not invest). If the lottery fails, you would receive $9 (the amount you did not invest).

Example: if you invest $9 and the lottery succeeds, you would receive $64 (7x $9 + $1). If the lottery fails, you would receive $1.
